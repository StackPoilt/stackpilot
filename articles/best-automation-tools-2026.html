How to build your first automation stack

Don't try to automate everything at once. The most effective approach for small businesses is the "Three Automations" framework:

Automation 1: Lead capture → CRM

Every new lead from your website, ad campaigns, or referral sources should automatically land in your CRM with full context. Set this up first — it prevents leads from falling through cracks and gives you a single source of truth. HubSpot's free plan handles this natively. Or use Make to connect any form tool to any CRM.

Automation 2: Follow-up sequences

Every lead should receive a follow-up sequence that runs without your intervention. The timing (immediate, day 2, day 5, day 14) and content should be personalized based on what the lead cared about. Apollo handles this for sales outreach. HubSpot handles it for marketing. Both are worth the investment.

Automation 3: Weekly reporting

You should never manually compile a status report. Use Make or Zapier to pull metrics from your key tools every Monday morning and deliver a summary to your inbox or Slack. This alone saves 2–3 hours/week for most business owners.
